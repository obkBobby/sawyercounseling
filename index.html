The process

See the pattern. Say the truth. Choose differently.

Therapy with me is warm and collaborative, but it is not passive. We work together to develop insight, name the cycle, and practice real change.

01

Identify the Pattern

We name what keeps repeating, whether it shows up as overthinking, shutdown, people pleasing, anger, conflict, or avoidance.

02

Understand the Cycle

We explore what the pattern protects, where it comes from, how it affects your relationships, and why it has been hard to change.

03

Practice New Responses

You learn to communicate more clearly, regulate your emotions, set boundaries, repair conflict, and make choices that align with who you want to become.

About Robert

Experienced therapy that feels human, direct, and practical.

I am Robert Sawyer, LMHC, a Licensed Mental Health Counselor with 18 years of experience helping individuals, couples, and families understand themselves, improve communication, and change the patterns that keep them stuck.

My style is warm, direct, collaborative, and insight oriented. Clients often describe the work as honest, grounding, and practical. You will have space to feel seen, but we will also pay attention to what needs to change outside of session.

I draw from CBT, solution focused therapy, family systems, attachment informed work, IFS informed work, Gottman informed couples work, emotionally focused principles, and trauma informed care. The point is not to bury you in therapy language. The point is to help you understand yourself and your relationships in a way you can actually use.

Questions clients ask

Before you book.

Do you offer online therapy?

Yes. Sawyer Counseling offers online therapy across Florida.

What if my partner is unsure about therapy?

That is common. A consultation can help clarify whether couples therapy is the right next step and what both partners would need to participate honestly.

Is therapy only for crisis?

No. Therapy can help when you are overwhelmed, but it can also help when you are functioning and still feel stuck, disconnected, or unclear about how to move forward.

Who is this not a fit for?

This is not crisis care. Couples therapy is also not appropriate for active domestic violence, high conflict custody disputes, or situations where one partner is attending only to punish or control the other.
